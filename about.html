Our Story

From a single hive to a flourishing sanctuary

Founded in 2015 by third-generation beekeeper Elara Wren, GoldenHive began with 12 rescued hives in Sonoma County. Today we steward over 400 hives across regenerative wildflower meadows, ensuring every drop of honey echoes the terroir of California's pristine landscapes.

Our mission bridges tradition and innovation — solar-powered extraction, no chemical treatments, and habitat corridors for native pollinators.

Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To nurture thriving pollinator ecosystems by practicing ethical, transparent beekeeping — delivering raw, unprocessed honey that preserves nature's purity while empowering local communities with regenerative agriculture education.

Our Vision

A future where every harvest strengthens the bond between people and pollinators, creating a global network of carbon-negative apiaries, thriving wildflower corridors, and a world where honey tells the story of ecological harmony.

Our Journey

Milestones that shaped our apiary and impact

2015

First Hive, First Drop

GoldenHive founded with 12 rescued hives in Sonoma, starting the journey of ethical honey production and rewilding.

2017

Regenerative Expansion

Expanded to 200+ hives & partnered with organic farms to create pollinator corridors across 500 acres.

2019

Solar-Powered Extraction

Launched first solar-powered honey extraction facility, reducing carbon footprint by 70%.

2022

Community Beekeeping School

Opened education center, training 300+ new beekeepers and donating 5% of profits to pollinator protection.

2025

Carbon-Negative Apiary Network

Achieved net-negative carbon status & protected 2,000+ acres of wildflower habitats across California.
